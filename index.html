01 / Seed

Pick one clip.

Choose one 10-second moment that demonstrates a tactical pattern — a press break, a switch of play, a build-up under pressure.

02 / Embed

The model converts it to a signature.

The model — trained on expert-labelled examples — turns the clip's player and ball trajectories into a tactical signature. No video is processed; only the tracking data.

03 / Retrieve + rerank

Top similar moments come back.

The system pulls 50 candidates by similarity in the model's embedding space, then reranks them using the analyst's tactical taxonomy (mechanism, zone, lane, pressure context, key roles). The final top-5 is returned — all from different matches, so geometric similarity from "same game" can't fake it. Both the embedding and the reranker are honest parts of the system.

how to read the results

Two things on each retrieved clip.

Strong / Partial / Miss

A post-hoc honesty check, not the model's output and not pre-existing ground truth. After the model returned its top 5, we compared each retrieval's tactical category (assigned by a football expert) against the seed's category:

  • STRONG retrieval is the same category as the seed
  • PARTIAL sister category (overlapping mechanism)
  • MISS different category — the model's confidence may still be high, but the analyst would say no

The labels grade the model's output; the model never saw them.

SIMILARITY
78%

The model's only output. A cosine-similarity score between the seed's and the retrieval's embedding — how alike the model thinks they are.

  • ~85–95% very high — model is confident
  • ~60–80% moderately similar — typical top hit
  • below ~50% weakly similar — closer to baseline

High similarity + miss badge = the model is confident but wrong. That's a useful signal.

where this is

Honest about the stage.

This is a phase-0 research demo, not a customer-ready product. The retrievals you see above are real — qualitative tactical-similarity output on expert-labelled clips. The harder claim — that the model generalises this quality to fresh, unseen analyst pairs — is not yet established. That's the next milestone.

  • Built on expert-labelled examples, demo is qualitative.

    The retrievals you see come from a labelled bank: every clip has an analyst tag. The model is doing a real job here, but on data the analyst has already touched.

  • Generalisation to fresh expert-labelled pairs is the next milestone.

    We have built the first eval discipline and learned that scaling expert-labelled, system-blind data is the core unlock. Onboarding a second analyst and labelling a held-out set is the immediate next step.

  • The corpus is small — n=10 matches, n=75 expert pair-judgments, one analyst.

    Phase 0. The wins shown are real on this corpus. Assertions about broader performance require more analysts, more matches, and a properly held-out evaluation — all in flight.

what's next

This is the foundation for finding recurring tactical problems and the clips that prove them. A coach asks "show me every press we broke down the left this season" and the system answers with five video clips. Today's demo is the embedding layer that makes that possible.
